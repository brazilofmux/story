# Articles

Longer-form writeups produced from the project's work. Different
genre from the design sketches in `design/` (which record the
design's reasoning-over-time, with numbered supersession) and
different from the research notes in `research/` (which survey
prior theories and systems external to this project). Articles
distill—they take the accumulated evidence in the repo and
present it for a specific audience at a specific moment.

## Convention

- Files named `YYYY-MM-DD-{slug}.md`. The date is the draft
  date (or first public post date if published); the slug is
  short and descriptive.
- Articles are **not** sketches. They commit to a narrative for
  a specific audience, not to an engineering or theoretical
  commitment. When the project's state evolves past what an
  article claims, the article stays in place as a record of
  what the author thought at that moment; subsequent articles
  may update the frame.
- Published articles (Substack, conference proceedings, etc.)
  note the publication URL in a footer or header. The repo
  version is the source / historical record; the published
  version is the canonical surface a reader cites.
- Editorial discipline is the same as the rest of the repo: no
  CI, no linters, editorial review at commit time.
