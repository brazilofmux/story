# The Warning

I am not Joseph Goebbels. But I think I have built something he would have recognized — not its scale, but its method: the treatment of meaning as a thing that can be specified, measured, and corrected until it lands.

I spent twenty years studying the deep structural grammar of story. After five months of work this year, the core of it finally worked.

Be precise about what *worked* means, because the precision is the whole point. Automated story **generators** are not rare — there is a small graveyard of them, and every large language model writes passable prose on command. That is not what this is. What is rare — what I am not aware of a true prior for — is **accountability**: a system that holds a generated story to a verifiable structural specification, finds the places where the thing fails to carry the meaning its author intended, repairs them, and then reads the result back *blind* to confirm the meaning landed — across four different theories of structure, without ever being told the answer.

It works on stories expressed in its own terms, not on any text you hand it. It grades its own work with the same kind of intelligence that made it. It has blind spots, and they are documented. By the standard of what people imagine when they hear "AI that understands story," it is small.

It is also enough to see the shape of what comes next.

Here is the honest gap, because a warning you cannot check is just noise. Today, with a human in the loop, this gets you a first draft. With more human help, you can refine that draft into something that holds an audience — something that might make money. It is a very long way from writing a *Communist Manifesto*, or founding a religion, or engineering a conviction someone would die for. That distance is real, and I will not pretend it isn't.

But the tool will improve. The whole class of tool will improve. And the distance will narrow. I am standing at the *beginning* of that process, not the end — and that is exactly why I am writing this now.

That is the warning. Not that I have built the weapon. That I have built the first cheap, measurable, repeatable *step toward* it — and that I can see the road.

Storytelling is not only art. It is one of the base protocols of human civilization — how we decide what is true, who is *us*, what is worth dying for. We do not reason our way to most of our convictions. We are told into them. A tool that can specify and correct the deep mechanics of story is a tool that can shape what people experience as true — and the trajectory points at doing it cheaper, more measurably, and more repeatably than the people doing it by hand today.

And they are doing it by hand today. Three-letter agencies, advertising machines, political operations — running cruder versions of this right now, at enormous expense, with results they cannot fully predict. What I built is a step toward making that capability cheap, measurable, and repeatable. The step is small. The direction is not in doubt.

Yes, I am happy I built it. That happiness came with a dark choice about what to do with it.

I am releasing it fully open source, MIT licensed, with complete documentation of what it can do and — just as importantly — what it cannot.

Not because I think it is harmless. Because the alternative is worse, and because *now* is the only honest time. The capability is coming regardless of what I do. The only real question is whether it arrives as a monopoly or as a commons — and that question can only be answered at the beginning, while the thing is still small enough to study and early enough for the public to learn to see it before it matures. Wait until the gap closes and only two outcomes remain, both worse: you release something genuinely dangerous, or you discover the monopoly already formed and the move already being made on people who never got to watch it happen.

A society where only the powerful understand how narrative weapons are built is not safer than one where everyone does. It is just a society where the powerful have already won, and no one else can see the move.

The defense against engineered meaning is not ignorance. It is literacy. You cannot resist a structure you cannot perceive. The only durable protection I can think of is to put the blueprint in everyone's hands at once — to make meaning-making something the public understands and practices, not something done *to* them by people who studied it in private.

In this new world, when something feels emotionally right, suspect that it was built to. Stories make reality realer than real. That is entertaining, sometimes a survival trait, and sometimes a weapon.

So here it is, while it is still small. Use it. Study it. Learn to see the bones of the stories already being told at you, every day, by people who would prefer you couldn't.

Weaponize it if you want. But don't ever say nobody warned you.
