# Design

Architectural sketches and design notes for the story-telling engine. Each sketch is numbered and dated; revisions do not overwrite predecessors. Old sketches are left in place as a record of how the design changed, so the reasoning over time is preserved.

## Convention

- Files named `{topic}-sketch-NN.md` where NN is sequential per topic. A *topic* is a coherent design area — `substrate`, `architecture`, `descriptions`, and so on. Each topic has its own sequence.
- Every sketch has a **Status** (*draft, active*, *superseded*, *abandoned*) and a **Date** at the top.
- **Active sketches are self-contained within their topic.** A reader should be able to understand the current state of *that topic* from the active sketch alone, without chasing a chain of deltas. When corrections pile up, consolidate into a new sketch rather than let the active starting point depend on superseded context.
- **Cross-topic sketches are expected.** Some sketches (notably architecture-level ones) apply across topics. They do not supersede the sketches they constrain; they add a frame that future topic sketches are expected to satisfy. Reading a topic sketch alone gives the reader that topic's current state; reading the cross-topic sketches additionally gives them the frame those topic sketches sit inside.
- When a sketch is superseded, its status is updated and a link to the successor is added — but the file stays.
- Sketches are not specifications. They are the design thinking in progress. Claims and commitments may be wrong. Open questions are first-class.

- [Dramatica template — sketch 01](dramatica-template-sketch-01.md) — depth probe of the Dramatic dialect by encoding Dramatica's full Grand Argument Story theory as a specific Template (`dramatica-complete`) within the dialect. Worked example: Oedipus Rex partially encoded against `dramatica-complete`, with the canonical Outcome=Success × Judgment=Bad = Personal Tragedy derivation shown. The sketch's load-bearing output is its *Where the dialect resists* section, surfacing six concrete forcing functions for a future dramatic-sketch-02: Template-level schema extension (M5 is too thin to carry Dramatica as-is); derived-field semantics for Solution/Symptom/Response; Story-level Template extensions; per-Function Throughline-assignment constraints; heavy Template theory-data (~300+ labels); quad-shape typed validators. Sandwich question (whether anything sits between Dramatica and substrate) explicitly left open.
- [Lowering — sketch 01](lowering-sketch-01.md) — design exercise (exploratory, findings-heavy rather than commitment-heavy) attempting a concrete lowering from the dramatica-complete Oedipus encoding down to the `oedipus.py` substrate encoding, to pressure architecture-sketch-02's lowering claim with a real case. Central finding: upper-to-lower coupling comes in four distinct kinds — **Realization** (upper record made true by specific lower records; uses Lowering per A7), **Characterization** (upper record classifies a pattern the substrate exhibits; uses verification per A8), **Claim** (upper record asserts something about substrate state at a moment or across a trajectory; uses verification per A8), and **Flavor** (free-form author metadata; no formal check). Lowering records are the right mechanism for Realization *only*; the temptation to fold every upper-to-lower relationship into Lowering is wrong. Findings F1–F7: the four coupling kinds (F1); Realization targets are heterogeneous — typed records, record sets, descriptions — while query-shaped couplings belong to the verifier surface not to Lowerings (F2); some upper records couple via the description surface (F3); position correspondence between upper and lower dialects is author-declared (F4); substrate gaps surface concretely — Tiresias, Creon, post-anagnorisis plot beats are missing from `oedipus.py` (F5); Lowering (A7) and Verification (A8) are genuinely different relationships, neither reducible to the other (F6); derivation composes with verification — the inference-01 rule surface is what makes many Claim-coupling checks tractable (F7). Answers the sandwich question: direct Dramatica→substrate coupling works for most upper records; a middle dialect (Structural) would make some Realization-couplings more natural but is not strictly required. Hands a concrete brief to a future `lowering-record-sketch-01` (seven requirements, now refined by the four-kinds distinction) and names follow-on actions including extending `oedipus.py` to cover the cut plot beats and running a cross-boundary reader-model probe.
- [Lowering — sketch 02](lowering-sketch-02.md) — second lowering exercise parallel to sketch 01, using *Macbeth* as a structurally different test case (action-first MC, moral rather than epistemic trajectory, multiple Antagonist readings, supernatural Throughline participants, cumulative rather than momentary Judgment=Bad). Sketches both upper (dramatica-complete) and lower (substrate, as prose — no `macbeth.py` yet) encodings just deeply enough for the coupling walk. Central result: the four-kinds framework from sketch 01 held up; deliberately probed three candidate fifth kinds (counterfactual, promissory, negative) and all decomposed into combinations of the existing four. New finding F8: **Claim coupling has two sub-kinds** — moment-pattern (Outcome=Success at τ_s = end) and trajectory-pattern (Argument's resolution across the whole narrative; Judgment=Bad as a gradual descent). Both require verifier primitives but the shapes are different. F1–F7 all generalized from Oedipus; F6 and F7 strengthened by Macbeth's reliance on trajectory-shape verification and derivation-composed checks. Implications: `lowering-record-sketch-01` is ready to draft (confidently scoped to Realization only); `verification-sketch-01` as a sibling should specify both moment-pattern and trajectory-pattern primitives; dialect-level schemas should declare coupling kind per record type. Next actions named: draft the record-shape sketches, author `macbeth.py`, extend `oedipus.py`, and (deferred) run a third exercise on a structurally-dissimilar-again story (comedy / ensemble / lyric).
- [Lowering record — sketch 01](lowering-record-sketch-01.md) — synthesis sketch informed by the two lowering exercises; specifies the Lowering record's exact shape. Commitments L1–L10: Realization coupling only (L1 is the guardrail); record shape with id, `upper_record` and `lower_records` as `CrossDialectRef` pairs, annotation with descriptions-01-style attention, optional `position_range` for author-declared position correspondence (per F4), `status ∈ {active, pending}` admitting unrealized Lowerings as first-class (per F5), staleness via `anchor_τ_a`; many-to-many binding; lower side admits typed-fact or description targets interchangeably; authored through the proposal queue, never synthesized; contains no verification logic (L10 defers verification to its sibling sketch). Sister to verification-sketch-01; the two form the load-bearing synthesis pair.
- [Verification — sketch 01](verification-sketch-01.md) — sibling synthesis sketch covering the coupling kinds Lowering does not: Characterization and Claim (moment-pattern and trajectory-pattern per F8). Commitments V1–V8: automated linting at dialect boundaries (V1); output via the existing proposal queue as observations, never errors (V2); three primitive verifier kinds — Characterization, Claim-moment, Claim-trajectory (V3); verifier speaks upper dialect vocabulary, evaluates via lower dialect queries (V4); coupling kind declared per record-type at the dialect schema level (V5); composes with inference-01's derivation surface for query-shaped checks (V6); partial-match observations first-class with match_strength signal (V7); architecturally distinct from Lowering (V8). Three output record shapes: `VerificationReview`, `StructuralAdvisory`, `VerificationAnswerProposal` — all flow through the existing proposal queue. Explicitly distinguishes cross-boundary verification (this sketch's topic) from *within-dialect* constraint propagation — the "Dramatica-16,384-forms click-into-place" phenomenon belongs to dialect self-verification or a Template-constraint sketch, not here. The two are complementary; they share the proposal queue but handle different concerns.

## Upcoming sketches

Planned work. Each is a sketch-to-write; filenames are provisional. Dependency order is approximate; sketches are advanced when a forcing function argues for it (e.g., the descriptions surface shipped write-only, which advanced reader-model; Jocasta's anagnorisis had a clean failure mode, which advanced identity-and-realization; the `project_reader` weakening had a clean forcing function in both encodings, which advanced focalization; the Oedipus parricide/incest encoding exercised the compound-predicate pinch, which advanced the inference model).

1. **Implementation pass for the Lowering + Verification pair** — now that the synthesis sketches (lowering-record-sketch-01, verification-sketch-01) are drafted, a first tooling-level implementation can add the `Lowering` record type, the three verifier primitives, the new queue entry kinds (`StructuralAdvisory`, `VerificationAnswerProposal`), and extend `proposal_walker.py` to walk them. Not a design sketch — concrete prototype work.
2. **Per-record-type coupling-kind declarations** — verification-sketch-01 V5 specifies that dialects declare, per record type, which coupling kinds apply (e.g., `Character → Realization`, `Argument.resolution_direction → Claim (trajectory)`, `Argument.domain → Flavor`). dramatic-sketch-01 and dramatica-template-sketch-01 do not yet carry these declarations. Lands either as an amendment to those sketches or as a new short sketch; a small but load-bearing item.
3. **Implement inference-model-sketch-01 in the substrate** — add `Rule`, `holds_derived`, `derive_all`, and proof-carrying derivation described in that sketch; retire the author-asserted compound predicates in `oedipus.py` (parricide/incest) and `macbeth.py` (kinslayer/regicide/breach_of_hospitality/tyrant). Concrete prototype work. Per verification-sketch-01 V6, many verifier checks consume derived substrate facts, so this closes a cross-sketch dependency.
4. **Extend `oedipus.py` substrate encoding** (concrete prototype work) — add Tiresias and Creon as Entities; add the cut plot beats (tiresias-accusation, jocasta-suicide, self-blinding, exile) as events + sjuzhet entries. lowering-sketch-01's F5 finding shows that these gaps block full lowering of the Dramatica-complete Oedipus encoding.
5. **Cross-boundary reader-model probe** (provisional) — feed the probe the upper encoding + lower encoding + a set of proposed Lowerings and have it emit reviews + edit proposals on the Lowerings. First real exercise of the reader-model probe at a boundary other than substrate/description, per architecture-sketch-02 A11. The sister Q between probe and verifier (verification-sketch-01 OQ4) — whether they coordinate and how — becomes concrete once both are live.
6. **Third lowering exercise on a structurally-dissimilar-again story** (deferred) — Oedipus and Macbeth share tragic-trajectory shape. A comedy (*A Midsummer Night's Dream*), an ensemble (*The Great Gatsby*, *The Wire*), or a tonal/lyric piece (*To the Lighthouse*) would pressure the framework in directions neither Oedipus nor Macbeth touched. Most valuable after the record-shape sketches are implemented; confirming or breaking the framework once verifiers run carries more weight than more design-level exercises.
7. **Dramatic dialect — sketch 02** (provisional) — addresses dramatica-template-sketch-01's six forcing functions. **Also the likely home for Template-level constraint-propagation** — Dramatica's "16,384 valid story forms" with the "click-into-place" authoring experience (per discussion around verification-sketch-01) is a within-dialect concern, not cross-boundary verification. Priority depends on whether dramatica-complete needs to be implementable soon.
8. **Structural dialect — sketch 01** (provisional, lowered-priority) — parallel upper dialect to Dramatic. Both lowering exercises confirmed direct Dramatica→substrate coupling works; a middle Structural dialect remains useful but not required. Stays provisional until a concrete case shows it earning its keep.
9. **Inference model — sketch 02** (provisional) — follow-ons to inference-sketch-01 as forcing functions emerge. Likely candidates: typed inequality / NAF for `sibling`-family rules; rule-anchored descriptions; rule attention; refining N9 under culturally-loaded compound predicates.
10. **View vs. sjuzhet visibility** (provisional, banked from the Oedipus probe walk) — the `ReaderView` exposes substrate state (τ_s, τ_a) but not the sjuzhet schedule (τ_d). Descriptions that reference disclosure-schedule coordinates ("the reader has held both since τ_d=0") make claims the view cannot verify. See `D_view_cannot_see_τ_d` in the Oedipus encoding.

The reader-model probe now exercises the view against live Claude Opus 4.6 (`reader_model_client.py`, `demo_reader_model.py` for Rashomon, `demo_reader_model_oedipus.py` for Oedipus), ingests structured reviews, answer proposals, and edit proposals as substrate-native records, and surfaces a proposal queue the author can walk interactively (`proposal_walker.py`, driven by `--walk`). The walker routes accept decisions through `ingest_review`, `accept_answer_proposal`, and `accept_edit_proposal`; declines through `decline_proposal`. Edit acceptance supersedes the source description (marks it `DescStatus.SUPERSEDED`, hides from default views) and commits a new record at a fresh τ_a, with `supersedes` / `superseded_by` metadata pointers preserving the chain — closing the bounded case of descriptions-sketch-01 OQ1 (description versioning) and reader-model-sketch-01 OQ6 (description-edit proposals). Persistence of the queue across sessions is still deferred per descriptions-sketch-01 OQ3.

Inference-model-sketch-01 was seeded by a probe walk against the Oedipus encoding: the probe's answer to `D_parricide_incest_authored_not_derived` supplied the derivable-boundary criterion (N9). The next unit of prototype work is implementing the `Rule` record, `holds_derived` / `derive_all` query surface, and proof-carrying derivation described in that sketch, then retiring the Oedipus encoding's author-asserted `parricide` / `incest` compound predicates and re-probing to see whether the probe reaches for richer rule families (sibling, uncle) that would pressure OQ1 or OQ3.
